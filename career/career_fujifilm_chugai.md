### 8. PK / PD Basic Notes (English + Japanese)

- **PK (Pharmacokinetics)**
  - What the body does to the drug.
  - Focuses on how the **drug concentration changes over time**.
  - Typical processes:
    - Absorption（吸収）: from administration site into the bloodstream
    - Distribution（分布）: from blood to tissues
    - Metabolism（代謝）: chemical transformation, mainly in the liver
    - Excretion（排泄）: elimination from the body, mainly via kidney
  - Typical quantities:
    - C(t): drug concentration over time
    - Cmax: maximum concentration
    - AUC: area under the concentration–time curve
    - t1/2 (half-life): time required to halve the concentration

- **PD (Pharmacodynamics)**
  - What the drug does to the body.
  - Focuses on the relationship between **drug concentration and effect**.
  - Typical ideas:
    - Effect E: response (e.g. biomarker change, symptom relief)
    - Emax: maximum effect
    - EC50: concentration achieving 50% of Emax
    - Hill coefficient γ: steepness of the concentration–effect curve
  - Example model (Emax model / Hill model):
    - \( E = E_{\max} \cdot \frac{C^\gamma}{EC_{50}^\gamma + C^\gamma} \)

- **PK–PD combined model**
  - PK model outputs C(t).
  - PD model takes C(t) as input and outputs E(t).
  - In drug development, PK–PD models are used to:
    - explore dosing regimens（投与量・投与間隔の検討）
    - predict efficacy and safety（有効性・安全性の予測）
    - support trial design（試験デザインの最適化）
